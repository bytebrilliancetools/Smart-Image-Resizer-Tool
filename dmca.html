SmartImageResizer ("we," "us," or "our") respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects our users to do the same. This DMCA Policy outlines our procedures for addressing copyright infringement claims in accordance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act of 1998 (17 U.S.C. § 512).

1. Overview

SmartImageResizer provides a client-side image resizing tool that processes images entirely within users' web browsers. We do not host, store, or have access to user images. However, we take copyright infringement seriously and will respond appropriately to valid notices of alleged copyright infringement.

2. Important Notice About Our Service

2.1 Client-Side Processing

Our image resizing tool operates using client-side technology, which means:

  • All image processing occurs locally in the user's web browser
  • Images are never uploaded to our servers
  • We do not store, cache, or have access to any user images
  • We cannot monitor what images users process
  • We cannot remove or block specific images from being processed

2.2 User Responsibility

Users are solely responsible for ensuring they have the legal right to upload, modify, and use any images processed through our service. By using our service, users represent and warrant that they own or have obtained all necessary rights, licenses, and permissions for the images they process.

3. Scope of DMCA Compliance

Given the nature of our service, DMCA takedown requests may apply to:

  • Content we host on our website (text, graphics, logos, etc.)
  • User-generated content we may host (such as contact form submissions, if published)
  • Any cached or stored content on our servers

DMCA takedown requests cannot apply to:

  • Images processed by users through our client-side tool (as we have no access to them)
  • Content that exists solely on user devices

4. Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

4.1 Requirements for Valid Notice

If you believe that content hosted on our website infringes your copyright, you may submit a DMCA takedown notice. To be valid, your notice must include the following information as required by 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3):

  1. Identification of the copyrighted work: A description of the copyrighted work that you claim has been infringed. If multiple works are covered by a single notification, provide a representative list.
  2. Identification of the infringing material: A description of where the material that you claim is infringing is located on our website. Please provide sufficient detail (e.g., URL) to enable us to locate the material.
  3. Contact information: Your full legal name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
  4. Good faith statement: A statement that you have a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  5. Accuracy statement: A statement that the information in the notification is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that you are authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.
  6. Physical or electronic signature: Your physical or electronic signature (typing your full name is acceptable).

5. Our Response to Valid Notices

5.1 Review Process

Upon receipt of a valid DMCA notice, we will:

  • Review the notice to ensure it meets statutory requirements
  • Investigate the alleged infringement
  • Take appropriate action if the claim is valid
  • Respond to the complainant within a reasonable timeframe

5.2 Actions We May Take

If we determine that content hosted on our website infringes copyright, we may:

  • Remove or disable access to the infringing material
  • Notify the user who posted the content (if applicable)
  • Terminate accounts of repeat infringers
  • Take other appropriate legal actions

6. Counter-Notification

6.1 If Your Content Was Removed

If you believe that content you posted was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification, you may file a counter-notification pursuant to 17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(3).

6.2 Requirements for Valid Counter-Notice

A valid counter-notification must include:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature
  2. Identification of the material that has been removed or disabled and the location where it appeared before removal
  3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification
  4. Your name, address, and telephone number
  5. A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or if outside the U.S., any judicial district in which we may be found)
  6. A statement that you will accept service of process from the person who provided the original DMCA notice or an agent of such person

6.3 How to Submit Counter-Notice

Send your counter-notification to:

Email: dmca@smartimageresizer.com

Subject Line: DMCA Counter-Notification

6.4 Counter-Notice Process

Upon receipt of a valid counter-notification:

  • We will forward it to the original complainant
  • We may restore the removed content after 10-14 business days
  • We will not restore content if the original complainant files a court action seeking an injunction

8.1 Penalties for Misrepresentation

  • Material or activity was removed or disabled by mistake or misidentification

shall be liable for any damages, including costs and attorneys' fees, incurred by the alleged infringer or the service provider who is injured by such misrepresentation.

8.2 Good Faith Requirement

We reserve the right to seek damages from any party who submits a DMCA notification or counter-notification in bad faith or with knowledge of material misrepresentation.

9.2 Safe Harbor
